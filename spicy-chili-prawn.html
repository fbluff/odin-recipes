Shrimp in Spicy Chili Sauce

Description

Before I proceed there is one crucial ingredient that makes this dish perfect. It is the stinky bean.Stinky bean or known as petai is one of the weirdest and stinkiest bean around. However, the tanginess of the beans compliments the sweet and spicy of the sauce or sambal. That what makes this dish the most loved dish to Malaysians. To anyone out there that is bold, fearless and adventurous, this dish is for you ♥

Ingredients
  • 400g shrimp
  • 100g petai
  • 4 tablespoon cooking oil
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1 Yellow onions
  • 1 Red onions
  • 75g dried chilies
Instructions
  1. Wash dried chilies and cut stems away. Remove the seeds to prevent the chilies from being bitter and spicy.

  2. Boil the cleaned dried chilies for 7 minutes drain them.

  3. Blend the chilies with red onions.

  4. Pour blended mixture and cook until the paste dries, around 15-20 minutes. It splashes! So use oil splash guard. Stir now and then.

  5. Once paste dries, add a cup of water to prevent burnt and stir for 1 minute.

  6. Add stink beans and shrimp. Cook them for another minute.

  7. For garnish, add thinly sliced yellow onion.

  8. Turn off the heat and serve immediately with steamed white rice.
